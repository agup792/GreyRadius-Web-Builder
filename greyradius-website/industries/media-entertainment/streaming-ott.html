Our POV · 2026

Streaming and ott platform consulting

The global streaming and OTT market is entering a new phase – moving from growth-at-all-costs to sustainable economics, market-specific content strategies, and profitable subscriber acquisition. Streaming platforms, FAST channel operators, sports streaming services, and regional OTT players are all evaluating new market entry opportunities as global platforms struggle to localise effectively. GreyRadius helps streaming businesses validate audience demand, navigate content and regulatory requirements, execute GTM plans, and raise capital.

Why now? Southeast Asia and South Asia represent the last major frontier for streaming platform growth – with 800 million smartphone users who are increasingly consuming video on mobile but are price-sensitive and language-diverse. The streaming platforms that invest in local language content and affordable pricing models in 2024–2027 will establish the dominant positions in these markets.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

The global streaming market is projected to reach $330B by 2030 – with Southeast Asia and South Asia growing at 15%+ annually, driven by affordable smartphones, cheaper mobile data, and rising demand for local language content.

Ad-supported video on demand is outgrowing subscription VOD in emerging markets – consumer willingness to watch advertising in exchange for free content is creating a viable AVOD business model that SVOD platforms are now adopting.

Sports streaming rights are the most powerful subscriber acquisition tool – platforms with exclusive sports rights in key markets are achieving subscriber growth 3–5x faster than entertainment-only platforms.

AI-powered content recommendation and dubbing is accelerating content localisation – platforms that can affordably dub or subtitle content into local languages at scale are achieving significantly higher engagement and retention.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • Content localisation cost is the primary market entry barrier – producing or acquiring local language content requires significant investment before subscriber economics are established.
  • Password sharing crackdowns are slowing subscriber growth in mature markets – platforms must balance revenue per household growth with user experience to avoid subscriber churn.
  • Advertising technology infrastructure is underdeveloped in many emerging markets – programmatic advertising revenue potential is lower than in mature markets, affecting AVOD business model viability.
  • Piracy remains a significant competitor in Southeast Asia and South Asia – illegal streaming services that offer free access to premium content create persistent subscriber acquisition headwinds.
