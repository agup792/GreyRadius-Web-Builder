Gulf smart mobility and urban transport market entry strategy

The Gulf is building the world's most ambitious smart mobility infrastructure – NEOM's autonomous transport system, Dubai's autonomous mobility vision targeting 25% autonomous transport by 2030, Abu Dhabi's smart mobility investments, and Saudi Arabia's transport infrastructure projects all creating unprecedented procurement for autonomous vehicles, connected mobility platforms, smart traffic management, and urban transport technology. International smart mobility companies across autonomous vehicle technology, connected vehicle platforms, smart traffic management, urban air mobility, and mobility-as-a-service are all evaluating Gulf market entry. GreyRadius helps smart mobility companies validate Gulf government and authority demand, access Vision 2030 and Smart City programme procurement, identify partners, and execute entry.

Why now? NEOM's autonomous transport system procurement is active – The Line's fully autonomous transportation is in active technology procurement in 2024–2027. Dubai's Roads and Transport Authority is procuring autonomous mobility technology under its Smart Mobility Strategy. The smart mobility companies establishing Gulf government relationships in this window will capture long-term infrastructure contracts.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

Gulf smart mobility market is projected to reach $8B by 2030 – driven by NEOM autonomous transport, Dubai Smart Mobility Vision, and Saudi Arabia's National Transport Strategy all creating sustained technology procurement.

NEOM's transportation system will be the world's first fully autonomous urban transport network – creating demand for autonomous vehicle technology, traffic AI, and mobility platform infrastructure at unprecedented scale.

Dubai has deployed autonomous marine transport, drone delivery trials, and autonomous taxi pilots – making it the world's most active city for advanced mobility technology deployment and validation.

Gulf governments are investing in public transport technology – Riyadh Metro, Doha Metro, and Abu Dhabi bus system all digitalising creating smart transit and mobility-as-a-service technology demand.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • Smart mobility procurement in Gulf is heavily government and authority-driven – Dubai RTA, NEOM, and Saudi transport authorities all control mobility technology procurement through complex government processes.
  • Autonomous vehicle regulatory frameworks are still developing – Gulf autonomous vehicle regulations are being developed in real-time as technology deployment accelerates creating regulatory uncertainty.
  • Technology demonstration and proof-of-concept requirements – Gulf authorities require demonstrated technology performance in Gulf climate and operational conditions before full deployment commitment.
  • Competition from established mobility technology companies – Waymo, Mobileye, and Alstom – with Gulf government relationships is growing as awareness of Gulf smart mobility opportunity increases.
