Our POV · 2026

Embedded insurance market entry strategy

Embedded insurance — insurance products distributed through non-insurance platforms at the point of need — is one of the most significant transformations in the insurance industry. Device insurance embedded in electronics retail, travel insurance in booking platforms, gig worker insurance in ride-hailing apps, and SME insurance in banking platforms are all creating significant market opportunities. Embedded insurance technology providers, API insurance companies, and insurance-as-a-service platforms are all evaluating new market entry opportunities across Southeast Asia, South Asia, and the Gulf. GreyRadius helps embedded insurance businesses validate platform demand, navigate insurance regulatory requirements, execute GTM plans, and raise capital.

Why now? Southeast Asian super apps — Grab, Gojek, and Sea Group — have demonstrated that embedded insurance can achieve extraordinary scale through platform distribution. Financial services and insurance penetration across Southeast Asia and South Asia is growing rapidly as mobile distribution makes insurance accessible for the first time. The embedded insurance infrastructure companies that establish platform relationships in 2024–2027 will power significant insurance premium volume.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

Global embedded insurance market is projected to reach $700B in gross written premium by 2030 — growing at 25% annually as non-insurance platforms across mobility, commerce, and healthcare all add embedded insurance products.

Device and product insurance embedded in e-commerce is growing at 35%+ — consumers purchasing electronics, appliances, and high-value goods through e-commerce are increasingly opting for embedded extended warranty and insurance at checkout.

Parametric insurance embedded in travel and weather platforms is growing rapidly — automatic, trigger-based insurance payments that require no claims process are achieving high consumer satisfaction and renewal rates.

Micro-insurance embedded in mobile money is expanding access — life, health, and agricultural micro-insurance distributed through mobile money platforms is reaching populations that have never previously been insured.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • Insurance product design for embedded distribution requires simplification — complex insurance terms and conditions create consumer friction at the point of purchase that reduces conversion rates.
  • Regulatory compliance for embedded insurance is complex — the platform distributing insurance may require an insurance broking licence, the insurer must be authorised in each market, and cross-border embedded insurance faces regulatory complexity.
  • Claims management for embedded insurance affects consumer experience — the moment of claiming is when embedded insurance creates or destroys consumer trust, requiring robust and frictionless claims processes.
  • Adverse selection risk in embedded channels is elevated — consumers opting into insurance at the point of purchase may have higher loss exposure than the general population, requiring careful underwriting design.
