Online gaming and igaming market entry strategy

Online gaming and iGaming — including online casino, sports betting, lottery, and poker platforms — is one of the most regulated but fastest-growing digital entertainment sectors globally. Online gaming operators, sports betting platforms, lottery technology providers, and affiliate marketing companies are all evaluating new market entry opportunities as markets regulate and licence online gaming, creating legal and compliant market entry pathways. GreyRadius helps online gaming businesses validate player demand, navigate licensing requirements, execute GTM plans, and raise capital.

Why now? Online gaming regulation is spreading globally — India, Brazil, Africa, and multiple Southeast Asian markets are all in various stages of establishing regulatory frameworks for online gaming. The companies that secure operating licences in newly regulated markets before competition intensifies will hold durable market positions. Regulatory windows in newly legalising markets typically remain open for 12–24 months before major incumbents capture market share.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

Global online gaming and iGaming market is projected to reach $130B by 2027 — growing at 12% annually as market regulation expands and mobile gaming adoption drives player acquisition.

Sports betting is the largest and fastest-growing iGaming segment — cricket betting in South Asia, football betting in Southeast Asia, and American football betting in new markets are all driving significant revenue growth.

Brazil's online gaming legalisation is the largest new market opening in a decade — creating a $2B+ annual iGaming market that is attracting significant operator investment and licence applications.

Responsible gaming technology is becoming a regulatory requirement — player protection tools including self-exclusion, deposit limits, and problem gambling detection are required for licence approval in most regulated markets.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • Gaming licence requirements are complex, expensive, and slow — operating licences for online gaming require capital deposits, technical system certification, corporate structure requirements, and regulatory review that takes 12–24 months.
  • Payment processing for online gaming is challenging — many banks and payment processors restrict gaming merchant accounts, requiring specialist payment solution providers.
  • Player acquisition cost is high and rising — digital advertising for online gaming is expensive, and acquisition of profitable players in competitive markets requires sophisticated CRM and bonus management.
  • Regulatory compliance is ongoing and intensive — licensed operators must maintain compliance with responsible gaming requirements, AML obligations, and technical system standards continuously.
