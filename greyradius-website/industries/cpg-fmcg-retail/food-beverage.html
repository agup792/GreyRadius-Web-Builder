Our POV · 2026

Food and beverage market entry consulting

The food and beverage industry is one of the most globally active market entry sectors — with international food brands, restaurant chains, food manufacturers, and F&B technology companies all expanding across Southeast Asia, South Asia, the Gulf, and Africa. GreyRadius helps food and beverage businesses validate consumer demand, navigate regulatory requirements, identify distribution partners, execute GTM plans, and raise capital — grounded in primary research with consumers, retailers, food service operators, and regulators.

Why now? The Gulf's food import dependency and premium consumer segment create significant opportunity for international food brands. Southeast Asia's rapidly growing middle class is creating first-time buyers of packaged, branded, and premium food products. India's food market is one of the fastest-growing consumer markets in the world. The food brands establishing distribution infrastructure in these markets in 2024–2027 will hold durable positions.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

Asia Pacific food and beverage market is projected to exceed $3T by 2027 — growing at 7% annually as rising incomes, urbanisation, and changing dietary preferences drive packaged and branded food adoption.

Healthy and functional food is growing at 12%+ annually — consumers across Southeast Asia, South Asia, and the Gulf are increasingly seeking food products with clear health positioning and clean label formulations.

Foodservice recovery and expansion is accelerating — restaurant chains, quick service restaurants, and food delivery platforms are all expanding aggressively across emerging markets.

Private label food is growing — regional supermarket chains are developing their own food brands to compete with international brands on price and quality, creating competitive pressure for branded food companies.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • Food regulatory approval varies significantly — import licences, product registration, labelling requirements, and ingredient approval differ across markets and require market-specific compliance investment.
  • Cold chain infrastructure gaps limit product range — fresh, chilled, and frozen food products require cold chain distribution that is underdeveloped in secondary cities across most emerging markets.
  • Distribution fragmentation is the primary GTM challenge — the mix of modern trade, traditional trade, and e-commerce channels differs by market and requires different distribution approaches.
  • Consumer taste preferences are highly local — food products that succeed in one market may require significant reformulation or flavour adaptation to achieve consumer acceptance in a new market.
