India manufacturing market entry strategy

India is the world's fastest-growing major manufacturing destination – with the Production Linked Incentive scheme offering $26B in subsidies across 14 sectors, 100% FDI permitted in most manufacturing sectors, and a government committed to making India a global manufacturing hub. International manufacturers across electronics, pharmaceuticals, automotive, textiles, chemicals, and food processing are all evaluating India manufacturing investment. GreyRadius helps international manufacturers validate Indian market and export demand, navigate PLI scheme access, identify local partners, and raise capital for India manufacturing investment.

Why now? The PLI scheme application windows are time-limited – electronics, pharmaceutical, and several other sector PLI schemes have active application windows in 2024–2026 that offer 4–6% production-linked incentives. Companies that secure PLI approval in these windows will have significant cost advantages over later entrants. China+1 supply chain diversification is creating urgency for India manufacturing investment decisions.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

India's PLI scheme has attracted $35B+ in manufacturing investment commitments across electronics, pharmaceuticals, food processing, and automotive sectors – creating supply chain opportunities for component and raw material suppliers.

India's electronics manufacturing is growing at 25% annually – Apple's India manufacturing expansion, Samsung's production scale-up, and Dixon Technologies' contract manufacturing growth are all creating supplier ecosystem development opportunities.

India's pharmaceutical manufacturing is the world's third-largest by volume – producing 20% of global generic medicine supply and creating significant API and pharmaceutical equipment supplier demand.

India's logistics cost is declining – dedicated freight corridors, multimodal logistics parks, and highway network expansion are reducing manufacturing logistics cost and improving supply chain competitiveness.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • PLI scheme eligibility requirements are complex – minimum investment thresholds, incremental sales requirements, and sector-specific local content rules all require careful assessment before application.
  • Labour regulations across Indian states vary – employment laws, factory act compliance, and labour dispute resolution differ across states, requiring state-specific regulatory assessment.
  • Supply chain development for quality inputs is challenging – India's manufacturing ecosystem has gaps in precision components, specialty chemicals, and advanced materials that require either import or local development investment.
  • Infrastructure quality varies significantly across states – power reliability, road connectivity, and port access differ dramatically between India's manufacturing clusters.
