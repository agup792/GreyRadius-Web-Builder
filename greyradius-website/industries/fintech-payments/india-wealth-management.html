India wealth management and private banking market entry strategy

India’s wealth management market is one of the world’s most significant and fastest-growing – with 350,000 High Net Worth Individuals holding $1T+ in investable assets, a growing ultra-HNI segment investing internationally, SEBI’s Registered Investment Adviser framework creating regulated wealth advice infrastructure, and India’s stock market outperformance creating strong equity wealth creation. International private banks, wealth management platforms, family office service providers, and investment advisory businesses are all evaluating India as a wealth management market. GreyRadius helps wealth management companies validate Indian HNI demand, navigate SEBI regulatory requirements, identify distribution partners, and execute market entry.

Why now? India’s HNI population is growing at 11% annually – one of the fastest HNI wealth creation rates globally as equity market outperformance, real estate appreciation, and entrepreneurial exits all create new wealthy individuals at scale. The wealth management companies establishing Indian HNI relationships in 2024–2027 will build AUM that compounds as India’s wealth creation continues.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

India’s wealth management market is projected to reach $1.5T in AUM by 2027 – growing at 15% annually as HNI population grows, international investment appetite increases, and regulated wealth advisory scales.

India has 350,000+ HNIs with $1T+ in investable assets – significantly underpenetrated by international wealth management given the sophistication of Indian HNI investment knowledge and risk appetite.

SEBI’s RIA framework is professionalising wealth advisory – creating regulatory infrastructure that international wealth management companies can use to establish credible, compliant India operations.

India’s ultra-HNI international investment appetite is growing significantly – Indian HNIs are increasingly interested in offshore equity, real estate, and alternative assets as portfolio diversification.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • SEBI registration requirements for wealth management are mandatory – RIA registration, investment adviser qualification, and compliance requirements all apply to wealth management businesses operating in India.
  • Indian HNI trust is relationship-driven – wealth management relationships in India depend on personal trust built over years through demonstrated performance and cultural credibility.
  • Competition from established Indian wealth managers – Motilal Oswal, Edelweiss, and ICICI Private Banking – with deep HNI relationships is intense across all segments.
  • LRS and FEMA restrictions affect Indian HNI offshore investment – the Liberalised Remittance Scheme $250,000 annual limit and FEMA compliance requirements affect the international investment proposition for Indian HNIs.
