India space technology market entry strategy

India's space technology market is undergoing a historic liberalisation – with IN-SPACe creating India's first private space regulatory framework, ISRO opening its launch facilities, infrastructure, and technology to private companies, and a $8B+ Indian space economy target by 2033. International satellite manufacturers, launch service providers, space data analytics companies, satellite communication businesses, and space technology component suppliers are all evaluating India as a space technology market entry destination. GreyRadius helps space technology companies validate Indian government and commercial demand, navigate IN-SPACe regulatory requirements, identify ISRO and commercial partners, and raise capital.

Why now? India's space liberalisation is creating an immediate commercial window – IN-SPACe has approved 140+ entities in its first two years, ISRO's commercial arm NewSpace India is actively seeking international partners, and the Indian government's 2033 space economy vision is creating funded demand. The space technology companies establishing Indian relationships in 2024–2027 will benefit from early market access before competition intensifies.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

India's space economy is targeted to reach $44B by 2033 – growing from $8B currently as satellite services, launch services, and space technology manufacturing all scale under IN-SPACe liberalisation.

India's PSLV and GSLV launch vehicles offer competitive commercial launch services – ISRO's NewSpace India is actively marketing launch services to international satellite operators at competitive prices.

India's earth observation satellite market is growing at 20% annually – government infrastructure monitoring, agricultural monitoring, and disaster management are all creating significant EO data demand.

IN-SPACe has approved 25+ private satellite companies and 10+ launch vehicle startups – creating a domestic space ecosystem that international companies can partner with or supply.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • IN-SPACe approval requirements are mandatory before space activities – all space operations, satellite launches, and space data services require IN-SPACe authorisation under the Indian Space Policy 2023.
  • Defence and dual-use technology export controls affect space technology transfer to India – ITAR, EAR, and Indian defence procurement regulations all affect space technology licensing.
  • ISRO partnership is strategically valuable but complex to establish – ISRO's limited commercial capacity and government procurement processes require patience and relationship investment.
  • Competition from Indian space startups – Skyroot, Agnikul, Pixxel, and 140+ IN-SPACe approved entities – is growing with deep ISRO relationships and domestic market knowledge.
