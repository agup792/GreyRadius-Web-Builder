Our POV · 2026

Hotels and resorts consulting

Hotel and resort development and expansion remains one of the most capital-intensive and operationally complex segments in hospitality. Hotel groups evaluating new geographies, resort developers assessing investment viability, and hospitality technology companies seeking hotel operator clients all need rigorous primary research and execution-ready strategy. GreyRadius helps hotel businesses validate demand, assess project feasibility, navigate regulatory requirements, and raise capital – grounded in primary research with guests, operators, developers, and investors.

Why now? The Gulf's tourism infrastructure investment – Saudi Arabia's NEOM, Red Sea Project, and Diriyah development – is creating one of the largest hotel development opportunities in history. Southeast Asia's post-pandemic tourism recovery is creating hotel demand that exceeds supply in several markets. Medical tourism growth is driving demand for specialised hospital-adjacent hotel infrastructure.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

Global hotel investment is projected to reach $30B annually by 2027 – with the Gulf, Southeast Asia, and South Asia receiving disproportionate investment driven by government tourism programmes and rising middle-class travel.

Extended stay and serviced apartment demand is growing faster than traditional hotels – remote work patterns and longer leisure travel trips are increasing demand for self-catering accommodation with hotel services.

Wellness and medical tourism hotels are commanding significant premiums – hotels with integrated spa, medical check-up, and wellness programme offerings are achieving ADR premiums of 30–50% versus comparable conventional hotels.

Hotel technology investment is accelerating – property management system upgrades, revenue management automation, and contactless guest experience technology are receiving significant capital allocation from major hotel groups.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • Hotel development timelines are extremely long – from site acquisition to opening typically takes 3–7 years depending on market and construction complexity, requiring long investment horizons.
  • Labour availability and training for hotel operations is a persistent challenge – skilled hospitality workforce is limited in many emerging markets, increasing training investment and operational cost.
  • Online travel agency commission dependency is creating margin pressure – OTA commission rates of 15–25% are significantly compressing hotel profitability and creating urgency for direct booking capability investment.
  • Sustainability requirements are intensifying – major corporate travel programmes and international hotel brands are requiring ESG compliance from properties, creating compliance investment requirements.
