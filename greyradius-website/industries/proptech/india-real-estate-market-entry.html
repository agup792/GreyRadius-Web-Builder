India real estate market entry strategy

India's real estate market is one of the world's largest and fastest-growing – with a housing shortage of 18 million units, rapid urbanisation creating first-time homebuyer demand, commercial real estate expansion driven by technology and manufacturing investment, and a PropTech ecosystem transforming how Indian property is discovered, transacted, and managed. International real estate developers, PropTech companies, construction technology businesses, and real estate service providers are all evaluating India market entry. GreyRadius helps real estate companies validate Indian market demand, navigate RERA and regulatory requirements, identify partners, and execute entry.

Why now? India's housing market is in a sustained upcycle – affordable housing demand from India's growing middle class, luxury housing demand from HNW investors, and commercial real estate demand from technology and manufacturing expansion are all simultaneously strong. The RERA framework has significantly improved real estate project transparency, increasing investor confidence in Indian real estate development.

Market Intelligence

What the data says.

India's real estate market is projected to reach $1T by 2030 – with residential, commercial, logistics, and data centre real estate all growing significantly across major Indian cities.

India's housing shortage of 18 million units represents the world's largest addressable residential real estate investment opportunity for developers and investors.

PropTech adoption in India is accelerating – NoBroker, Housing.com, and Square Yards have demonstrated the commercial viability of technology-driven real estate services in the Indian market.

India's commercial real estate is growing at 12% annually – technology company office expansion, manufacturing facility development, and warehousing growth all driving Grade A commercial demand.

Market Reality

What makes this market hard.

  • RERA compliance requirements vary across Indian states – Real Estate Regulatory Authority registration requirements, project disclosures, and escrow obligations differ across 30+ state RERA bodies significantly.
  • Land titling and acquisition is complex – unclear land titles, multiple owner structures, and acquisition processes require specialised legal expertise in each state.
  • Construction quality standards and delivery timelines are challenges – finding reliable construction partners that maintain quality and timeline commitments is critical for successful real estate development in India.
  • Foreign investment restrictions apply in some real estate categories – FEMA regulations restrict foreign investment in certain residential real estate structures requiring careful legal structuring in advance.
